UX Portfolios

UMSI CDO Philosophy on UX Portfolios

The UMSI Career Development Office (CDO) takes a pragmatic approach to portfolio development, encouraging students to aim for a portfolio that is 'good enough' to showcase your work and skills. While continuous improvements are encouraged, remember, there is no such thing as a perfect portfolio—just one that’s ready to share.

Additionally, connect with the Career Development Office to receive individualized feedback on your portfolio! We offer the following services to help you create your best UX portfolio:

  • Scheduled Career Coaching Appointments

    Our team can meet with you to discuss your internship/job search and career interests, review your cover letters, resumes, and portfolios, and even help prepare you for interviews.

    To schedule an appointment to meet with a CDO Career Coach, please use the following link: UMSI CareerLink

  • Virtual Drop-in Coaching

    Virtual drop-in coaching is an opportunity to ask quick career-related questions that can be answered in 5-10 minutes via Zoom. To ensure we are able to see all students who come to drop-in hours, students with more time-intensive questions may be asked to make an appointment.

    See the Events page on UMSI CareerLink for available times during the residential academic year

What is a User Experience (UX) Portfolio?

A UX portfolio is a curated collection of 3-5 UX projects, often presented as a website. It showcases your skills, process, and problem-solving abilities as a UX designer and/or UX researcher. Employers and clients use your portfolio to assess your qualifications and determine if you’re a good fit for their team or project. Your portfolio is your opportunity to highlight your growth, creativity, and unique approach to solving user problems. A strong portfolio emphasizes your design thinking process, storytelling, and the real-world impact of your work.
